# Vera5 Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** June 29, 2026
**Last Updated:** June 29, 2026

Vera5 is an open-source browser extension and cybersecurity analysis tool designed to detect and enrich security-related indicators within webpages and browser-based analyst workflows.

This Privacy Policy explains how Vera5 handles information when you:

* visit the Vera5 website at **https://vera5.io**;
* install or use the Vera5 browser extension;
* interact with Vera5 documentation, repositories, support channels, or related services; or
* use third-party threat-intelligence services through Vera5.

In this Privacy Policy, “Vera5,” “we,” “us,” and “our” refer to **[LEGAL OWNER OR ENTITY NAME]**, the operator and publisher of Vera5.

## 1. Summary of Vera5’s Privacy Approach

Vera5 is designed around the following principles:

* local-first operation;
* no Vera5 user account required for core functionality;
* no default telemetry or behavioral analytics;
* no Vera5-operated enrichment cloud required for core functionality;
* bring-your-own API keys;
* local storage of settings, credentials, preferences, and cached results;
* no sale of personal information;
* no sale or sharing of browsing history;
* no advertising profiles;
* no silent upload of full webpages;
* no transmission of API keys to Vera5-operated servers; and
* user control over which third-party intelligence sources receive queries.

When you choose to enrich an indicator, the selected indicator may be transmitted directly from your browser to the third-party provider or endpoint you enabled. Those providers process the indicator under their own terms and privacy policies.

## 5. Information Handled by the Vera5 Extension

### 5.1 Local Page Analysis

When activated or configured to scan automatically, Vera5 may inspect permitted webpage content within your browser to identify supported cybersecurity artifacts, such as:

* IP addresses;
* domains;
* URLs;
* file hashes;
* vulnerability identifiers;
* email addresses;
* autonomous system numbers;
* certificate or cryptographic information; and
* other supported security indicators.

This inspection is performed locally within your browser.

Vera5 does not transmit the complete webpage, browser tab contents, ticket contents, email body, dashboard contents, or browsing history to Vera5-operated infrastructure.

Detection does not necessarily cause an external network request. External enrichment occurs only according to your settings and actions.

### 5.2 Information Stored Locally

Vera5 may store information in browser extension storage on your device, including:

* extension preferences;
* enabled or disabled features;
* enabled intelligence sources;
* API keys supplied by you;
* domain policies or allowlists and denylists;
* cached enrichment results;
* cache timestamps;
* selected or detected indicators;
* investigation sessions;
* collections;
* notes;
* labels;
* watchlists;
* export settings;
* local workflow history;
* local AI endpoint settings; and
* other locally configured operational data.

This information remains on your device unless you:

* export it;
* copy it to the clipboard;
* save it to a file;
* transmit an indicator to an enabled provider;
* configure a local or self-hosted endpoint;
* install a browser-sync mechanism outside Vera5’s control; or
* use a future optional service that clearly discloses different handling.

Removing the extension may not automatically remove every exported file, copied item, browser backup, or data stored outside the extension’s local storage.

### 5.3 API Keys and Credentials

Vera5 uses a bring-your-own-key model.

API keys you provide are intended to be stored locally in browser extension storage or in a user-controlled local or self-hosted backend.

Vera5 does not intentionally:

* transmit your API keys to Vera5-operated servers;
* pool your API credentials with other users;
* resell access to your credentials;
* use your credentials for another user;
* publish your credentials;
* include your credentials in exported settings by default; or
* knowingly include credentials in logs or public reports.

API keys are necessarily transmitted to the applicable third-party provider when required to authenticate a request to that provider.

Although Vera5 applies safeguards such as masking and export exclusions, browser-local storage is not equivalent to dedicated hardware-backed secret storage. You are responsible for protecting access to your browser profile, device, backups, and API-provider accounts.

Vera5 does not control the provider’s data practices.
